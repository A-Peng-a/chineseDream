Hong kong-zhuhai-macao bridge A sea bridge in China connecting Hong Kong, zhuhai and Macao

  • Home page

Hong kong-zhuhai-macao Bridge, located in the area of lingdingyang, guangdong province, China, is a Bridge and tunnel project connecting Hong Kong, Zhuhai and Macao within the territory of China.

Hong kong-zhuhai-macao bridge starts from the artificial island of Hong Kong port near Hong Kong international airport in the east, and connects zhuhai and Macao artificial islands after the lingdingyang of the south China sea in the west, ending at the zhuhai hongwan interchange. The total length of the bridge and tunnel is 55 km, of which 29.6 km is the main bridge and 41.6 km is from the Hong Kong port to the zhuhai-macao port. The bridge deck is a two-way six-lane expressway with a designed speed of 100 km/h; [1] the total investment of the project is 126.9 billion yuan.

Hong kong-zhuhai-macao bridge is famous for its huge construction scale, unprecedented construction difficulty and top construction technology. The chief designer of the bridge project is meng fanchao, the chief engineer is su quanke, and the general manager and chief engineer of the island tunnel project is Lin Ming.

The construction process

The predecessor of HZMB is the lingdingyang bridge under the former planning. In the early 1980s, although the land transport channels between Hong Kong, Macao and mainland China were constantly improved, the transport links between Hong Kong and the west coast of the pearl river delta were restricted by the lingdingyang sea. In the late 1990s, affected by the Asian financial crisis, the government of the Hong Kong special administrative region (hksar) considered it necessary to build a cross-sea channel linking Hong Kong, zhuhai and Macao as soon as possible, so as to give full play to the advantages of Hong Kong and Macao and find new economic growth points.

The construction achievements

The hong kong-zhuhai-macao bridge project has the characteristics of large scale, short time limit, new technology, little experience, many procedures, wide range of professional, high requirements, and many difficulties. It is the longest sea-crossing bridge in the world and has high standards in road design, service life, collision and earthquake prevention, flood and wind resistance and other aspects. [53] during the construction of the hong kong-zhuhai-macao bridge, many Chinese universities and research institutes played an important role in providing technical support.

Leng xijia, project manager, said that the crane has self-sailing capacity and DP2 power positioning function. Compared with ordinary crane, the cost and towing time of the tug are saved and the operation is more accurate and free.

The architectural style design of the hong kong-zhuhai-macao bridge and tunnel gathers cultural elements from guangdong, Hong Kong and Macao, condenses the common cultural memory of guangdong, Hong Kong and Macao, and implies the joint efforts of guangdong, Hong Kong and Macao to build the "project of the century". Among them, qingzhou channel bridge, jianghai direct ship channel bridge and jiuzhou channel bridge respectively show the image of Chinese knot, three Chinese white dolphins and sailing boats. Part of the inspiration for the style of east and west artificial island architecture comes from lingnan architecture, such as "colonnade" and "arcade", etc. [77] the east and west artificial islands each have two bronze tripod bridgeheads. They depict four stories of "sea embroidery", "miracle of island building", "flood dragon sailing" and "full and full of dreams" in relief. They tell the important construction process of hong kong-zhuhai-macao bridge respectively. [125] one side of the east and west artificial islands is provided with large steps that extend from the bottom to the top, leaving room for six additional escalators that allow visitors to enjoy views at different heights, such as dolphin habitats, sea waves and sunrises and sunsets.
