Along with economic globalization, the world shipping market is rapidly moving toward integration and networking, and market competition in the world is becoming increasingly fierce. The focus of competition is also becoming more and more obvious. It is to compete for the status of shipping center in the global market and seize the commanding heights of shipping. In a country with a huge market and a vast hinterland in China, especially in the Yangtze River Delta, if there is no international shipping center, it will be in a passive position in the international division of labor and competition. To build the Shanghai International Shipping Center is to represent the country and actively participate in international division of labor and international competition. Shanghai is China's largest port city. The city is used by Hong Kong and Hong Kong. Shanghai has the conditions and the ability to undertake the important task of participating in the competition of international shipping centers.

Complete achievement

In 2010, Shanghai Port completed a container throughput of 29.07 million TEUs, surpassing Singapore for the first time to become the world's busiest container port. On December 23, 2014, the fourth phase of the Yangshan Deepwater Port Area of Shanghai International Shipping Center was officially started. It is the first fully automated container terminal in China with a total investment of approximately RMB13.9 billion. It is expected to be completed in 2017. By then, Shanghai Port's annual throughput will exceed 40 million TEUs. This figure is the total throughput of all ports in the United States and is one-tenth of the annual global port throughput.

The global share of China's port machinery equipment has increased from 70% five years ago to 82%. Looking at every section of the world's coastline, made in China, it can only be made in China and the whole world.
