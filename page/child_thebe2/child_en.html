The overland silk road The Silk Road

The overland silk road originated in the western han dynasty (202-8 BC). Zhang qian was sent by emperor wu of the han dynasty on a mission to the western regions. Starting from chang 'an (present xi 'an), the capital of the han dynasty, the silk road went through gansu and xinjiang to central Asia and west Asia, and connected the land routes of Mediterranean countries. Its original function was to transport silk produced in ancient China. In 1877, the geological geologist Mr Leahy also in his book, "China" and the "from 114 BC to 127 AD, between China and central Asia, China and India with silk trade as the medium of the western region traffic" named "silk road", the term was quickly accepted by the academia and the public, and formal.

The traditional silk road began in chang 'an, the ancient capital of China, and reached the Mediterranean Sea through central Asian countries, Afghanistan, Iran, Iraq and Syria. The road is considered to be a meeting point of ancient eastern and western civilizations that connected the continents of Asia and Europe, and silk was the most representative cargo. For thousands of years, nomads or tribes, merchants, churchmen, diplomats, soldiers and academic researchers traveled along the silk road.

Grassland in the silk road

The formation of the grassland silk road is closely related to the natural ecological environment. In the geographical environment of the whole Eurasian continent, communication between east and west is extremely difficult. Environmental archaeological data show that only mid-latitudes between 40 and 50 degrees north latitude in Eurasia, home to the prairie silk road, are conducive to east-west human transportation. This is the core area where nomadic culture and farming culture meet, and an important link point of the grassland silk road.

For the grassland silk road, the demand for bulk Commodity Exchange originated from the division of labor between agriculture and animal husbandry in the primitive society. The dry farming areas in the central plains are dominated by agriculture, rich in grain, hemp, silk and handmade products. The northern grassland areas mainly focus on animal husbandry, rich in cattle, horses, sheep and skin, wool, meat, milk and other animal products, but lack of food, textiles, handmade products. This relationship between the central plains and the grassland areas is mutually dependent and economically demanding, which is the basic condition for the formation of the grassland silk road [54]. Therefore, the grassland silk road was also called "fur road" and "tea road" due to its characteristics.

On June 22, 2014, the 38th world heritage conference held in doha, Qatar announced that the eastern section of the ancient silk road jointly declared by China and haji countries: "silk road: road network of chang 'an and tianshan corridor" was successfully declared a world cultural heritage, becoming the first project of transnational cooperation and world heritage application.

At the 38th UNESCO world heritage committee meeting in doha, Qatar, the grand canal project of China and the silk road project jointly declared by China, kazakhstan and kyrgyzstan were included in the world heritage list, becoming the 32nd and 33rd world cultural heritage sites of China. The silk road is China's first joint application for world heritage status.

Value influence

Cultural impact: Through the maritime silk road, China also spread its national crafts and Confucian and Taoist thoughts, exerting varying degrees of influence on the countries and regions along the "maritime silk road" as well as other parts of Europe, and even causing "China fever". Among them, porcelain and tea have a great influence on the world.

Historical impact: The collision of different cultures driven by the maritime silk road has promoted the progress and development of the world. Therefore, the open communication with an international perspective has become the ideological consensus of world development.

Economic impact: It has become a major maritime channel for trade and cultural exchanges between China and foreign countries and promoted the common development of countries along the routes
