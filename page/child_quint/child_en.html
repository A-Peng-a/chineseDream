Traditional Chinese medicine was born in primitive society. During the Spring and Autumn Period and the Warring States Period, the theory of traditional Chinese medicine was basically formed, and then it was summarized and developed in all dynasties. In addition, it has a far-reaching impact on the countries of Chinese character culture circle, such as Japanese Chinese medicine, Korean medicine, Korean medicine, Vietnamese medicine and so on, which are developed on the basis of traditional Chinese medicine.

Traditional Chinese medicine carries the experience and theoretical knowledge of ancient Chinese people's struggle against diseases. It is a medical theory system formed and developed gradually through long-term medical practice under the guidance of ancient simple materialism and spontaneous dialectics.

Based on the theory of Yin-Yang and Five Elements, TCM regards the human body as the unity of Qi, shape and spirit. Through the method of "seeing, hearing, interrogating and cutting" four diagnostic methods, it explores the causes, pathogenicity, location, analysis of pathogenesis and the changes of five zang-organs, meridians, joints, qi, blood and body fluid in human body, and judges the growth and decline of pathogenic factors, thus obtains the name of the disease.

Chinese MedicineCharacteristic

Theoretical system

Man is an integral part of nature, which is composed of Yin and Yang. Yin and Yang are opposite and interdependent, and they are moving and changing all the time. In normal physiological state, the two are in a dynamic balance. Once the dynamic balance is destroyed, it will be pathological.

Man and nature are a unified whole, that is, "the unity of heaven and man" and "the correspondence between heaven and man". The law of human life activities and the occurrence of diseases are closely related to various changes in nature (such as seasonal climate, regional areas, day and night, dusk, etc.). People live in different natural environments and people's adaptation to the natural environment is different, and their physical characteristics and pathogenesis are also different.

In the diagnosis and treatment of the same disease, more attention should be paid to adjusting measures to the time, place and person, which is not uniform. It is believed that all the tissues and organs of the human body coexist in a single entity, which is interrelated and interacted in physiology and pathology.

The Book of Difficulties

The book enriches and develops the theoretical system of traditional Chinese medicine with its academic views on Mingmen and Sanjiao, as well as the names of Qichongmen (seven key parts of digestive tract) and Bahui (the meeting place of zang, fu, tendon, marrow, blood, bone, pulse and qi).

On Febrile Diseases and Miscellaneous Diseases

Written by Zhang Zhongjing in the Eastern Han Dynasty. Zhang Zhongjing (150-219 A.D.) is a famous machine with the character Zhongjing, a native of Nanyang.

Shennong Herbal Classic

Also known as "Shennong Materia Medica", short for "Materia Medica Classic" and "Benjing", the earliest existing pharmacological monograph in China.

Huangdi Neijing

The earliest traditional medical works on physiology, pathology, diagnostics, therapeutic principles and pharmacology in China. Based on the theory of Yin-Yang, Five Elements and the unity of nature and man, the dissection, physiology and pathology of human body, as well as the diagnosis, treatment and prevention of diseases are comprehensively expounded.
