The maritime silk road (sea trade channel)

  • Home page
  • the Belt and Road
  • Maritime silk

Maritime silk road, is the ancient Chinese and foreign trade and cultural exchanges between sea lanes transportation, also known as the "road of the sea ceramics" and "road" sea salts, in 1913 by the French department of home sand Wan mentioned for the first time. The maritime silk road originated in the shang and zhou dynasties, developed in the spring and autumn period and the warring states period, formed in the qin and han dynasties, flourished in the tang and song dynasties, and changed to the Ming and qing dynasties. It is the oldest known maritime route. The China maritime silk road is divided into two routes: the east China sea route and the south China sea route, with the south China sea as the center.

The south China sea route, also known as the south China sea silk road, starts mainly in guangzhou and quanzhou. In the pre-qin period, lingnan ancestors opened a trading circle with ceramics as the link in the south China sea and even the south Pacific coast and its islands [2-3]. "Guangzhou tonghaiyi road" in tang dynasty was the earliest name of China's maritime silk road and the longest ocean shipping route in the world at that time. During the Ming dynasty, zheng he's voyages to the western seas marked the heyday of the maritime silk road. The south China sea silk road runs from China to indo-china peninsula and south China sea countries, crosses the Indian Ocean, enters the red sea, and reaches East Africa and Europe, passing through more than 100 countries and regions. It has become a major maritime channel for trade and cultural exchanges between China and foreign countries, and promoted the common development of countries along the route.

Historical background

The earliest known record of the maritime silk road exchanges between China and foreign countries comes from the book of han · geography records. At that time, China had contact with countries in the south China sea. The unearthed relics suggest that the sino-foreign exchanges may have been earlier than the han dynasty. Before the mid-tang dynasty, China's main route to the outside world was the overland silk road. Later, due to war and the shift of economic focus, the maritime silk road replaced land as the main route for sino-foreign trade exchanges. In the tang dynasty, there was a sea route called "guangzhou tonghaiyi road" along the southeast coast of China, which was the earliest name of China's maritime silk road. With a total length of 14,000 km, this route was the longest ocean route in the world at that time, passing through more than 100 countries and regions [9-10]. During the song and yuan dynasties, it was an important carrier of human historical activities covering half the earth and cultural and economic exchanges between east and west [11]. During the sui and tang dynasties, silk was the main bulk cargo transported by the sea passage, so it was called the maritime silk road later. In the song and yuan dynasties, the export of porcelain gradually became the main goods, so it was also called "the road of sea ceramics". At the same time, as a large part of the export of commodities are spices, so it is also known as the "sea spice road." The maritime silk road is a common name.

Modern development

Since its opening in the qin and han dynasties, the maritime silk road has been an important bridge for economic and cultural exchanges between the east and the west. Southeast Asia has been an important hub and part of the maritime silk road since ancient times. Based on history and with an eye on the new historical starting point of the 10th anniversary of china-asean strategic partnership, general secretary xi jinping has put forward the strategic concept of "21st century maritime silk road" in order to further deepen china-asean cooperation, build a closer community of common destiny and bring benefits to both sides and the people in the region. The maritime silk road in the 21st century strategic partners and not only limit and the association of south-east Asian nations (asean), but in point of belt line, in line with the surface, enhance exchanges with border of countries and regions, the string connected asean, South Asia, west Asia, north Africa, Europe and other major economic sector of the market chain, developing strategic cooperation for the south China sea, the Pacific and Indian Ocean economic belt, with asia-europe economic trade integration for the development of long-term goal. Since asean is located at the crossroads and only way through the maritime silk road, it will be the primary development goal of the new hsi strategy. However, China and asean have broad political basis and solid economic foundation, and the hsi strategy in the 21st century conforms to the common interests and requirements of both sides.

The east China sea route mainly goes to the Japanese islands and Korean peninsula, while the south China sea route mainly goes to southeast Asia and the Indian Ocean region. Before the song dynasty, the east sea route mainly came and went from ningbo, while the south China sea route mainly came and went from guangzhou.

Chinese goods were continuously exported to Japan and Korea, and Chinese culture spread to these countries on a large scale, including Confucianism, legal system, Chinese characters, costumes, architecture, tea drinking customs and so on. Chinese culture has exerted a profound influence on Japanese and Korean ethics, political system, literature and art, living habits, social customs and other aspects.

Value influence

Cultural impact: Through the maritime silk road, China also spread its national crafts and Confucian and Taoist thoughts, exerting varying degrees of influence on the countries and regions along the "maritime silk road" as well as other parts of Europe, and even causing "China fever". Among them, porcelain and tea have a great influence on the world.

Historical impact: The collision of different cultures driven by the maritime silk road has promoted the progress and development of the world. Therefore, the open communication with an international perspective has become the ideological consensus of world development.

Economic impact: It has become a major maritime channel for trade and cultural exchanges between China and foreign countries and promoted the common development of countries along the routes
